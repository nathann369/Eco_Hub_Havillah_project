ABOUT US

The School Climate Clubs were officially launched across Nigeria on 2 October 2020 by the Environmental Friendly Initiative in collaboration with the National Youth Service Corps, Abuja Secondary Schools Board, Nigerian Global Shapers, Nigerian Climate Reality Leaders, Coalition of Civil Society working on SDG13, Media and individual climate actors. That launch is now a School Eco-Clubs Community.

The Eco Clubs Community is a network of school communities, individuals, and organizations inspiring and leading an intergenerational agenda that recognizes the unique skills and perspectives that young people offer, incorporating this to secure a sense of ownership over actions that protect the integrity of the earth into the future.

We believe climate and environmental education is important to start early.

MISSION: Our mission is to catalyze local solutions to the climate and ecological crisis by making urgent actions a necessity across every sector of society led by young people.

What is Sustainability?
The term, sustainability can be defined as development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs. Sustainability is aimed at integrating three dimensions which are economic, environmental, and social (also socio-political).
About Conservation
We’ve observed that the conservation sector is beginning to make a deliberate effort to engage young people. Young people are, after all, due to inherit the earth. It is the nature of the earth to be inherited, however, which makes youth engagement more important now than at any time in history.

About The School Climate Clubs?
The School Climate Club was initiated in the context of Sustainable Development Goal 13 (SDG-13), Climate Action because we believe in the power of schools to change the world.
What is Climate and Global Change?
The Earth’s climate is changing and the scientific consensus concludes that by altering the composition of the atmosphere, humans are increasing the average temperature of the Earth's surface. This process has already begun; the planet is measurably warmer than it was at the start of the last century.
